A leadership team is not just a collection of capable individuals. It is a system — with its own dynamics, communication patterns, trust levels, and blind spots. And like any system, it can perform well below its potential even when every individual in it is talented.

Team coaching addresses the team as a whole. It works on the dynamics that either enable or undermine collective performance — and it does so in the context of the real work the team is doing, not in a workshop simulation disconnected from what actually matters.

Alexandra's team coaching work was featured in Forbes, where her engagement with a senior leadership team demonstrated how targeted, sustained coaching produced measurable shifts in collaboration, accountability, and organizational culture.

## When Teams Need Coaching

Team coaching is most effective when:

- Trust has broken down and the team is working at cross purposes
- A new leadership team is forming and needs to align quickly
- The team is technically capable but underperforming as a unit
- A significant change — new leader, new mandate, restructuring — has disrupted team cohesion
- The team has plateau'd and needs a genuine step-change in how it operates

## What the Work Looks Like

Team coaching with Alexandra is a sustained engagement, not a one-day offsite. Real change in team dynamics requires time, honest conversation, and the opportunity to try new ways of working and reflect on what's shifting.

A typical engagement involves:

- An initial assessment phase — understanding the team's current reality, history, and specific challenges through individual and collective conversation
- Facilitated team sessions that surface the real issues, not just the presenting ones
- Ongoing coaching between sessions to support integration and accountability
- Progress reviews with the team and with organizational leadership as appropriate

The pace and format are designed around your team's specific situation and schedule.

## What Changes

Teams that complete a coaching engagement with Alexandra consistently report:

- Improved trust and psychological safety — people say what they actually think
- Clearer roles, accountabilities, and decision-making norms
- More productive conflict — disagreement that leads somewhere rather than going underground
- Stronger collective performance on the things that actually matter
- A shared sense of direction and mutual commitment to it

## Who This Is For

This program is for senior leadership teams, project teams, and cross-functional groups where the quality of team dynamics is directly affecting organizational outcomes. If the team is capable but something is getting in the way, team coaching is almost always the most efficient path to a different result.
